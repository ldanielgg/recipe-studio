Carne Asada Street Tacos

Ingredients

• 1kg Flank or Skirt Steak

• Small Corn Tortillas

• Marinade: Lime juice, Garlic, Cilantro, Cumin, Oil

• Garnish: Finely diced White Onion and Fresh Cilantro

• 2 Limes, cut into wedges

• Radishes, thinly sliced (optional)

Step 1: The Acidic Marinade

• Combine lime juice, oil, garlic, and spices. Marinate the steak for 1 to 4 hours.

• Before cooking, remove from marinade and pat the surface dry.

Extra Details

The citrus juice acts as a chemical tenderizer, breaking down the tough fibers of the flank steak. However, don't exceed 4 hours, or the acid will turn the exterior of the meat mushy. Patting the meat dry is essential—if it's wet, it will steam in the pan instead of getting that charred, smoky crust.

Step 2: The High-Char Sear

• Heat a cast-iron skillet or griddle until it is screaming hot.

• Cook the steak for 3–5 minutes per side until charred on the outside but medium-rare inside.

Extra Details

Authentic street tacos rely on "bits" of charred meat. This caramelization provides the savory depth that defines Carne Asada. Use a high-smoke-point oil (like avocado oil) to avoid filling your kitchen with acrid smoke while achieving that intense sear.

Step 3: The Rest and Dice

• Let the steak rest for 5–10 minutes.

• Slice against the grain into thin strips, then rotate and dice into small 1/4 inch cubes.

Extra Details

Dicing the meat into small cubes increases the surface area, allowing any juices and lime juice added later to coat every single piece. Slicing "against the grain" (perpendicular to the muscle fibers) ensures that every bite is tender and easy to chew.

Step 4: Blister the Tortillas

• Dip a corn tortilla lightly in the meat juices (or a drop of oil) and place on the hot griddle.

• Heat for 30 seconds per side until slightly charred and pliable.

Extra Details

Corn tortillas are fragile when cold. Heating them on a dry or lightly oiled pan "cures" the masa, making them flexible so they don't crack under the weight of the fillings. Doubling up the tortillas is the traditional "street style" move—it prevents the taco from falling apart if the first layer gets soaked with salsa.

Step 5: Assemble and Garnish

• Place a generous spoonful of meat on the doubled tortillas.

• Top with the raw onion and cilantro mix. Serve with a lime wedge and radish slices.

Extra Details

In a true street taco, the meat is the star, and the onion/cilantro mix provides a sharp, fresh contrast to the heavy fat of the beef. No cheese, no lettuce, no sour cream—the beauty is in the restraint. Squeeze the lime over just before your first bite to brighten all the flavors.
