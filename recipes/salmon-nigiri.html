Salmon Nigiri (Sake)

Ingredients

• 300g Sushi Rice (Short-grain)

• 200g Sashimi-grade Salmon

• 50ml Rice Vinegar

• 15g Sugar & 5g Salt

• Wasabi paste & Soy sauce

Step 1: Prepare the Sushi Rice

• Wash rice until water runs clear. Cook with slightly less water than usual for a firmer grain.

• While hot, fold in the vinegar, sugar, and salt mixture using a slicing motion.

• Let the rice cool to body temperature under a damp cloth.

Extra Details

In Japan, the rice (Shari) is considered more important than the fish. Using a "slicing" motion with a wooden paddle prevents mashing the grains. The goal is "Shin-ari"—rice that is firm on the inside but fluffy on the outside.

Step 2: Slice the Salmon (Neta)

• Use a very sharp knife to cut the salmon across the grain.

• Aim for slices about 1cm thick, 5cm long, and 3cm wide.

• Keep the fish chilled until the exact moment of assembly.

Extra Details

Cutting against the grain breaks down the connective tissue, making the salmon melt in your mouth. Traditional chefs use a single, long pulling stroke. Avoid "sawing" the fish, as it bruises the delicate fats.

Step 3: Form the Rice Ball

• Dampen your hands with "Tezu" (vinegar water) to prevent sticking.

• Pick up roughly 20g of rice and gently shape it into a small oblong cylinder.

• Do not squeeze too hard; the rice should hold together but remain airy.

Extra Details

A perfect nigiri ball should contain enough air that if you dropped it into water, it would float. If you pack it too tightly, the texture becomes gummy and masks the flavor of the salmon.

Step 4: The Assembly

• Place a slice of salmon in your left palm and dab a tiny amount of wasabi in the center.

• Place the rice ball on top of the salmon.

• Use your thumb and fingers to press the sides and top, molding the fish around the rice.

Extra Details

The wasabi acts as "glue" between the fish and rice. When pressing, use a light touch. The warmth of your hands should just barely begin to soften the salmon fats without "cooking" the exterior.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

• Lightly brush the top with a tiny amount of soy sauce or leave plain.

• Serve immediately with pickled ginger (Gari) on the side.

Extra Details

Nigiri is designed to be eaten in one bite. If dipping in soy sauce, flip the piece so only the fish touches the liquid. Dipping the rice will cause the nigiri to fall apart and over-salt the grain.
