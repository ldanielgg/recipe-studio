Pan-Seared Ribeye Steak

Ingredients

• 1 Thick-cut Ribeye (at least 1.5 inches)

• 2 tbsp Neutral Oil (Grapeseed or Avocado)

• 4 tbsp Unsalted Butter

• 3 Garlic cloves, smashed

• 3 sprigs Fresh Thyme or Rosemary

• Flaky Sea Salt & Coarse Black Pepper


Step 1: The Dry Brine

• Pat the steak bone-dry with paper towels on all sides.

• Season heavily with salt and pepper. Let it sit at room temperature for 45 minutes.

Extra Details

Salt acts as a dry brine. Initially, it draws moisture out, but after 40 minutes, the meat reabsorbs that seasoned liquid, breaking down muscle fibers for a more tender bite. If you cook it just 10 minutes after salting, the moisture will stay on the surface and prevent a good sear.

Step 2: The High-Heat Sear

• Heat a cast-iron skillet over high heat until the oil is shimmering and just starting to smoke.

• Carefully lay the steak into the pan, laying it away from you to avoid oil splashes.

• Sear for 2-3 minutes without moving it until a deep crust forms.

Extra Details

Cast iron is preferred because of its thermal mass; it won't drop in temperature when the cold meat hits it. You are looking for the Maillard reaction—a chemical reaction between amino acids and reducing sugars that gives browned food its distinctive flavor.

Step 3: Flip and Render the Fat

• Flip the steak. Use tongs to hold the steak vertically to sear the thick fat cap on the side.

• Sear the second side for another 2 minutes.

Extra Details

Ribeyes have a significant "fat tail" and an eye of intramuscular fat. If you don't render the fat cap on the side, it remains chewy and unappealing. Searing the edge melts that fat, which then acts as further cooking oil for the rest of the steak.

Step 4: The Arrosé (Butter Basting)

• Turn heat down to medium. Add butter, garlic, and herbs to the pan.

• Tilt the pan so the foaming butter pools at the bottom. Use a large spoon to continuously drench the steak in the hot butter.

Extra Details

This French technique, *Arrosé*, adds a nutty, aromatic depth that you can't get on a grill. The foaming butter acts as a high-heat conductor, finishing the internal cooking of the meat while keeping the crust hydrated and flavorful.

Step 5: The Rest

• Remove the steak when it reaches 130°F (54°C) for medium-rare.

• Let it rest on a warm plate or cutting board for 10 full minutes.

Extra Details

Resting is non-negotiable. The internal temperature will continue to rise about 5 degrees (carryover cooking). More importantly, the internal pressure drops, allowing the juices to redistribute throughout the meat. Slice it too early, and you lose all that moisture to the board.

Step 6: Slice and Finish

• Slice against the grain into thick strips.

• Pour the remaining brown butter from the pan over the slices and finish with flaky sea salt.

Extra Details

Slicing against the grain shortens the muscle fibers, making each bite more tender. The brown butter from the pan is packed with umami and herbaceous notes that elevate the steak to a new level of indulgence. A final sprinkle of flaky sea salt adds a contrasting crunch and bursts of salinity that enhance the overall flavor.
