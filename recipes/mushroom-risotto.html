Wild Mushroom Risotto

Ingredients

• 300g Arborio or Carnaroli Rice

• 400g Mixed Mushrooms (Cremini, Porcini)

• 1.2L Warm Vegetable Stock

• 150ml Dry White Wine

• 50g Butter & 60g Grated Parmesan

• 2 Shallots, finely minced

Step 1: Sear the Mushrooms

• Sauté mushrooms in a very hot pan with oil until browned. Set aside.

Extra Details

If you cook mushrooms with the rice from the start, they become rubbery. Searing them at high heat first develops "umami" flavors through caramelization. We add them back later to preserve their texture.

Step 2: Tostatura (The Toast)

• Sauté shallots in butter. Add the dry rice and stir for 2 minutes until the edges are translucent.

Extra Details

Toasting the rice creates a hard shell that prevents the grains from breaking. This ensures the rice stays "al dente" (firm to the bite) even as the outside releases starch to make the sauce creamy.

Step 3: Deglaze

• Add the white wine. Stir constantly until the liquid is fully absorbed by the rice.

Extra Details

The acidity in the wine is essential to balance the heavy fats (butter/cheese) added at the end. It also helps loosen the starches on the surface of the grain.

Step 4: The Gradual Additions

• Add warm stock one ladle at a time, stirring frequently until each ladle is absorbed.

Extra Details

Stirring is mechanical work. The grains rubbing against each other knock the starch off, creating the sauce. If you add all the liquid at once, you’re just boiling rice; adding it slowly builds the emulsion.

Step 5: Incorporate Mushrooms

• Fold the seared mushrooms back into the rice during the last 5 minutes of cooking.

Step 6: Mantecatura (The Creaming)

• Remove from heat. Vigorously stir in cold butter and Parmesan cheese.

Extra Details

This MUST be done off the heat. The goal is to create a fat-in-water emulsion. If the pot is too hot, the butter will just melt into oil rather than becoming a creamy sauce.

Step 7: The Rest

• Cover the pot and let it sit for 2 minutes before serving.

Extra Details

The rest allows the temperature to even out and the sauce to "set." If it’s too thick, add a final tiny splash of stock until it achieves "All'Onda"—moving like a wave when you shake the pan.
