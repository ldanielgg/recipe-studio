Shakshuka

Ingredients

• 5-6 Large Eggs

• 1 Large Onion, diced

• 1 Red Bell Pepper, seeded and chopped

• 4 Garlic cloves, minced

• 800g Canned Whole Peeled Tomatoes (hand-crushed)

• Spices: 2 tsp Paprika, 1 tsp Cumin, 1/2 tsp Chili Powder

• Fresh Parsley and Cilantro, chopped

• 100g Feta Cheese, crumbled


Step 1: Sauté the Aromatics

• Heat olive oil in a deep skillet or cast iron pan over medium heat.

• Add the onions and bell peppers. Cook for about 8–10 minutes until they are soft and beginning to caramelize.

• Stir in the garlic and spices (paprika, cumin, chili) and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.

Extra Details

The secret to a rich shakshuka is the "sweetness" of the peppers and onions. Don't rush this stage; you want the vegetables to lose their crunch and form a jammy consistency. Toasting the dry spices in the oil (blooming) releases their fat-soluble flavors, creating a much deeper aromatic profile.

Step 2: Reduce the Sauce

• Pour in the crushed tomatoes and their juices. Season with salt and a pinch of sugar.

• Simmer uncovered for 15 minutes until the sauce thickens and the oil starts to separate at the edges.

Extra Details

You are looking for a thick, chunky sauce that can hold its own shape. If the sauce is too watery, the eggs will boil rather than poach, and the whites will spread too thin. The pinch of sugar is a traditional trick to balance the high acidity of the canned tomatoes.

Step 3: Create the Wells

• Use the back of a large spoon to make 5 or 6 small "wells" or indentations in the tomato sauce.

• Carefully crack an egg into each well.

Extra Details

By creating these "nests," you ensure the eggs stay in place and are surrounded by the heat of the sauce. For extra safety, crack each egg into a small ramekin first before sliding it into the sauce to avoid any broken yolks or stray shells.

Step 4: The Gentle Poach

• Turn the heat to low. Cover the pan with a lid.

• Cook for 5–8 minutes, or until the egg whites are set but the yolks are still runny.

Extra Details

This is the most delicate part of the process. The residual heat and steam trapped by the lid will cook the tops of the whites. Keep a close eye on them; the difference between a perfect runny yolk and a hard-boiled one is often just 60 seconds.

Step 5: Garnish and Dip

• Sprinkle the crumbled feta and fresh herbs over the top.

• Serve immediately in the pan with warm pita, challah, or crusty sourdough bread.

Extra Details

Shakshuka is meant to be communal. The bread isn't just a side; it's your utensil. The saltiness of the feta provides a perfect contrast to the warm, earthy spices of the sauce. Enjoy it while the yolks are warm and liquid gold.
