Quinoa Harvest Bowl

Ingredients

• 1 cup Quinoa (rinsed)

• 1 Sweet Potato (cubed)

• 2 cups Kale (shredded)

• 1 Apple (thinly sliced)

• goat cheese, pecans, and maple-dijon dressing

Step 1: The Dual Cook

• Roast cubed sweet potatoes at 400°F (200°C) with olive oil and cinnamon for 20 minutes.

• Simultaneously, simmer quinoa in water (1:2 ratio) for 15 minutes, then let it rest covered.

Extra Details

Starting these together is the key to efficiency. The 5-minute rest for the quinoa is non-negotiable; it allows the steam to finish the grain, ensuring it's fluffy rather than mushy. The cinnamon on the potatoes provides an earthy bridge between the savory kale and sweet apples.

Step 2: Massage the Base

• Rub shredded kale with a splash of olive oil and salt for 2 minutes until dark and soft.

• Whisk the maple syrup, Dijon, and apple cider vinegar to create the vinaigrette.

Extra Details

Massaging kale isn't just a trend—it's a chemical necessity. Physical friction breaks down the tough cellulose walls that make raw kale hard to digest and bitter. You are looking for the leaves to turn a vibrant, translucent forest green.

Step 3: Build the Bowl

• Layer the warm quinoa and kale, then top with roasted potatoes, apples, nuts, and cheese.

• Drizzle generously with dressing and serve immediately.

Extra Details

The "Harvest" style relies on a mix of temperatures. The warmth of the quinoa slightly melts the goat cheese, while the raw apples provide a cold, crisp snap. If you're packing this for later, keep the dressing separate to maintain the textural contrast.
