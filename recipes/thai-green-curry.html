Thai Green Curry

Ingredients

• 400ml Full-fat Coconut Milk (do not shake)

• 3-4 tbsp Green Curry Paste

• 500g Chicken Breast or Thigh (thinly sliced)

• 200g Bamboo shoots or Thai Eggplant

• 2 tbsp Fish Sauce & 1 tbsp Palm Sugar

• 5-6 Kaffir Lime Leaves (torn)

• Handful of Thai Basil leaves

Step 1: Crack the Coconut Cream

• Spoon the thick, solid coconut cream from the top of the can into a wok or large pan.

• Heat over medium-high until it bubbles, reduces, and the oil begins to separate (crack) from the solids.

Extra Details

In authentic Thai cooking, we don't use vegetable oil. We "crack" the coconut milk to use the natural coconut oil as our frying medium. You'll know it's ready when you see small pools of clear oil forming and it smells intensely of coconut. This is the foundation of a glossy, rich curry.

Step 2: Fry the Paste

• Add the green curry paste to the cracked oil.

• Fry for 2–3 minutes, stirring constantly, until the paste is fragrant and deeply green.

Extra Details

Frying the paste "blooms" the herbs—lemongrass, galangal, and green chilies. If you simply boil the paste in the milk, the flavor will be flat and raw. You want to fry it until the pungent aroma fills the room, but be careful not to burn the delicate shallots and garlic inside the paste.

Step 3: Season and Simmer

• Add the chicken and toss to coat in the paste. Cook until the exterior is no longer pink.

• Pour in the remaining coconut milk and a splash of water (or chicken stock).

• Stir in the fish sauce, palm sugar, torn kaffir lime leaves, and vegetables.

Extra Details

Thai cuisine is all about the balance of four flavors: Salty (fish sauce), Sweet (palm sugar), Spicy (chili in the paste), and Sour (lime added at the end). Tearing the kaffir lime leaves releases their citrus oils more effectively than slicing them would.

Step 4: The Final Infusion

• Simmer for 5–7 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and vegetables are tender-crisp.

• Turn off the heat. Stir in the Thai basil leaves immediately.

Extra Details

Thai basil is different from Italian basil; it has a distinct licorice or anise-like flavor. We add it at the very end because the residual heat is enough to wilt it. Boiling the basil would destroy its delicate aroma and turn the leaves black.

Step 5: Serve with Jasmine Rice

• Ladle the curry into bowls. Garnish with fresh chili slices or extra basil.

• Serve alongside steamed Jasmine rice to soak up the creamy sauce.
