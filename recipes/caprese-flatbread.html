Caprese Flatbread

Ingredients

• 1 Rectangular Flatbread (or Naan)

• 150g Fresh Mozzarella (sliced or pearls)

• 2 Large Heirloom Tomatoes (or Cherry tomatoes)

• Fresh Basil leaves

• Balsamic Glaze & Extra Virgin Olive Oil

• 2 Garlic cloves, peeled

Step 1: Prep the Base

• Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

• Lightly brush the flatbread with olive oil and toast for 3–5 minutes until just starting to crisp.

• Once out, rub the raw garlic cloves across the surface for a subtle aroma.

Extra Details

Rubbing raw garlic on toasted bread is a classic Italian technique called "bruschetta style." It provides a punchy flavor without the bitterness of burnt garlic. Don't skip the pre-toast; it prevents the tomato juices from making the bread soggy.

Step 2: The Tomato Foundation

• Slice the tomatoes evenly. If using cherry tomatoes, halve them.

• Pat the slices with a paper towel to remove excess moisture.

• Arrange them in a single layer over the flatbread, leaving a small border for the crust.

Extra Details

Heirloom tomatoes are preferred for their vibrant colors and varying acidity. Patting them dry is a "pro-move" that ensures your flatbread stays crisp rather than steaming in the oven. Season the tomatoes directly with a pinch of sea salt at this stage.

Step 3: Cheese & Melt

• Tear the fresh mozzarella into bite-sized pieces and distribute evenly.

• Return to the oven for 5–7 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and just beginning to gold.

Extra Details

Fresh mozzarella (especially Buffalo Mozzarella) has high water content. If it looks too wet, you can switch the oven to "Broil" for the last 60 seconds. This caramelizes the cheese quickly before the bread gets too hard.

Step 4: The Finishing Touches

• Remove from oven and let sit for 2 minutes.

• Scatter fresh basil leaves over the top. If the leaves are large, tear them by hand.

• Drizzle generously with balsamic glaze and a final splash of extra virgin olive oil.

Extra Details

Never bake the basil! The high heat will turn the leaves black and bitter. Adding them to the warm (but not scorching) flatbread allows the residual heat to release the basil's essential oils, creating that iconic Caprese aroma.

Step 5: Slice and Serve

• Use a pizza cutter or a large chef's knife to slice into triangles or strips.

• Add a final crack of black pepper and serve while the cheese is still stretchy.

Extra Details

A drizzle of honey or a sprinkle of red pepper flakes can add a modern twist to this classic. The contrast between the cold balsamic glaze and the hot bread is what makes this a perfect appetizer.
