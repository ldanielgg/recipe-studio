Beef Bourguignon

Ingredients

• 1.5kg Beef Chuck (2-inch cubes)

• 200g Lardons or thick-cut bacon

• 750ml Red Wine (Burgundy/Pinot Noir)

• 500ml Beef Stock

• 2 tbsp Flour & 1 tbsp Tomato Paste

• Pearl Onions & Button Mushrooms

Step 1: Render the Lardons

• Sauté the bacon lardons in a large Dutch oven over medium heat until golden and crispy.

• Remove the bacon with a slotted spoon, leaving the rendered fat in the pot.

Extra Details

Animal fat has a higher smoke point and more flavor than butter for the initial sear. We want to use this "liquid gold" to brown the beef, which creates a smoky base for the entire stew.

Step 2: The Batch Sear

• Pat the beef dry. Sear in the bacon fat in batches until a dark brown crust forms on all sides.

• Remove the meat and set aside. Avoid crowding the pan.

Extra Details

If you put all the meat in at once, the temperature drops and the meat steams in its own juices. We need the Maillard reaction—that deep brown crust is what provides the savory "umami" depth of the sauce.

Step 3: Aromatics & Singer

• Sauté sliced carrots and onions in the remaining fat.

• Stir in the flour (Singer method) and tomato paste. Cook for 2 minutes to toast the flour.

Extra Details

Toasting the flour directly with the fat and vegetables creates a roux. This ensures that when we add the wine, the sauce thickens naturally without forming lumps or tasting like raw flour.

Step 4: The Deglaze

• Pour in the red wine slowly, scraping the bottom of the pot to release the "fond."

• Add the beef stock, return the beef/bacon to the pot, and add the herbs.

Extra Details

The "fond" are those caramelized bits stuck to the bottom. The acidity in the wine dissolves them, incorporating that concentrated flavor directly into the liquid. This is the most important flavor-building step.

Step 5: The Oven Braise

• Cover and place in a 325°F (160°C) oven for 3 to 4 hours.

• The meat is ready when it is fork-tender and the collagen has fully melted.

Extra Details

A Dutch oven is ideal here because it distributes heat evenly from all sides. The low, steady temperature transforms tough connective tissue into silky gelatin, which gives the sauce its legendary body.

Step 6: Sauté the Garniture

• While the beef is in the oven, sauté pearl onions and mushrooms in butter until golden brown.

Extra Details

Cooking these separately prevents them from becoming grey and mushy. You want them to retain their distinct texture and a hit of fresh butter flavor when they hit the final plate.

Step 7: Sauce Reduction

• Remove the beef from the pot. Simmer the liquid on the stove until it thickens enough to coat a spoon.

• Skim any excess fat from the surface.

Step 8: Final Assembly

• Return the beef and add the mushrooms and onions. Toss gently to coat.

• Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.
