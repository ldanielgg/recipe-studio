Lemon Herb Roasted Chicken

Ingredients

• 2 Large Chicken Breasts (skin-on preferred)

• 2 Lemons (1 sliced, 1 juiced)

• 4 Garlic cloves, smashed

• Fresh Herbs: Rosemary, Thyme, and Oregano

• 60ml Extra Virgin Olive Oil

• Sea Salt & Coarse Black Pepper

Step 1: Temper and Season

• Remove chicken from the fridge 20 minutes before cooking.

• Pat the skin extremely dry with paper towels.

• Season generously on both sides with salt and pepper.

Extra Details

Cooking "cold" chicken causes the muscle fibers to seize, resulting in a tough texture. By tempering the meat to room temperature, you ensure even heat penetration. Furthermore, moisture is the enemy of crispiness; the drier the skin, the better the sear.

Step 2: The Skin-Side Sear

• Place chicken skin-side down in a cold skillet, then turn heat to medium-high.

• Sear for 5–7 minutes without moving the meat until the skin is golden and crisp.

• Flip the chicken once the skin releases easily from the pan.

Extra Details

Starting in a cold pan (the "duck breast method") allows the fat under the skin to render out slowly. This results in a glass-like crunch rather than a chewy, fatty layer. If you use a hot pan immediately, the skin might burn before the fat melts.

Step 3: Aromatics and Basting

• Add the lemon slices, smashed garlic, and fresh herb sprigs directly into the pan juices.

• Spoon the hot, flavored oil over the chicken repeatedly (basting) for 2 minutes.

Extra Details

Basting is like a "flavor bath." The oil carries the essential oils from the rosemary and garlic into the nooks and crannies of the meat. The lemon slices will begin to caramelize, losing their sharp bite and becoming sweet and jammy.

Step 4: Finish and Deglaze

• Add the lemon juice and a splash of water (or white wine) to the pan.

• Cover with a lid or transfer to a 400°F oven for 5–8 minutes until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).

Extra Details

Using a meat thermometer is the only way to guarantee juice chicken. The liquid creates a small amount of steam that finishes the interior of the breast gently without drying out the exterior you worked so hard to crisp.

Step 5: The Crucial Rest

• Transfer chicken to a board and let it rest for at least 5 minutes before slicing.

• Pour the remaining pan juices over the meat just before serving.

Extra Details

If you cut the chicken immediately, the juices will run out onto the board, leaving the meat dry. Resting allows the fibers to relax and reabsorb those citrusy, herbaceous juices. This is the difference between a "good" home meal and a "restaurant-quality" dish.
