Spaghetti Carbonara

Ingredients

• 200g Spaghetti

• 100g Guanciale

• 2 Whole Eggs

• 50g Pecorino Romano, finely grated

• Freshly cracked black pepper

Step 1: Cook the Pasta

• Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Salt it generously. It should taste like the sea.

• Add the spaghetti and cook until just al dente according to the package instructions.

• Reserve about 1 cup of pasta water before draining.

Extra Details

In Rome, they say the pasta water is your hidden ingredient. The starch in it is what binds everything together later, without cream and without butter. When salting the water, do not be shy. This is your only chance to season the pasta itself. Carbonara relies on balance, not heavy sauces.

Step 2: Render the Guanciale

• Place the guanciale in a cold skilley, then turn the heat to medium.

• Cook slowly until the fat renders out and the meat becomes golden and slightly crispy, about 5 to 7 minutes

• Turn off the heat once done, but keep the guanciale in the pan.

Extra Details

Starting with a cold pan allows the fat to render gradually instead of searing too quickly. Guanciale is cured pork cheek. It is richer and silkier than pancetta or bacon. If you listen closely, the sizzling sound changes when it is ready. It becomes sharper as the moisture cooks off and only fat remains.

Step 3: Make the Egg and Cheese Mixture

• In a bowl, whisk together:

• 2 whole eggs

• Finely grated Pecorino Romano

• A generous amount of freshly cracked black pepper

• Mix until smooth and creamy

Extra Details

The black pepper is not just seasoning. It is part of the identity of carbonara. Some say the dish was named after charcoal workers, referencing the specks of black pepper resembling coal dust. Use finely grated cheese so it melts seamlessly. Large shreds will not emulsify properly.

Step 4: Combine, The Crucial Moment

• Add the drained spaghetti directly into the pan with the guanciale while the heat is off. Toss to coat the pasta in the rendered fat.

• Wait about 30 to 60 seconds so the pasta cools slightly.

• Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta and toss vigorously.

• Add small splashes of reserved pasta water as needed, stirring constantly, until a silky, glossy sauce forms that clings to the noodles.

Extra Details

This is the moment that separates good carbonara from scrambled eggs with pasta. The residual heat of the pasta cooks the eggs gently into a creamy sauce. No cream is required. If it looks too thick, add a tablespoon of pasta water. If too loose, toss more and let it tighten. You are aiming for velvety, not watery. The sauce should coat each strand like satin.

Step 5: Finish and Serve Immediately

• Plate immediately. Top with extra Pecorino Romano and more cracked black pepper.

• Serve hot.

Extra Details

Carbonara waits for no one. It is best eaten immediately while the sauce is at peak silkiness. In traditional Roman trattorias, you will often see it served simply, with no parsley, no garlic, and no cream. The beauty is in restraint.
